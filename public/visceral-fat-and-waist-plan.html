Shane’s Core Goal: Lose Visceral Fat & Reach a 32" Waist

Goal

The true target is not just a number on the scale. Your real goal is:

  • Lose visceral fat from your midsection.
  • Measure that loss by:
    • Overall weight loss, and
    • Reaching about a 32" waist at the navel.

1. Visceral Fat vs Subcutaneous Fat

What’s Around Your Midsection

  • Subcutaneous fat: Fat under the skin, the stuff you can pinch.
  • Visceral fat: Deeper fat packed around organs, pushes the belly out, and is more strongly tied to health risks.

The combo you’re about to run (Lion diet → Whole Foods + weekly fasts) is exactly the type of environment where visceral fat tends to drop:

  • Consistent calorie deficit.
  • Low refined carbs and sugar.
  • Built-in fasting windows (Lion’s 1–2 meals/day, plus later weekly 36–40 hr fasts).

2. How to Track Progress: Waist First, Scale Second

Weekly Waist Measurement (Core Metric)

Once per week, same conditions:

  • Morning, after bathroom, before food or drink.
  • Stand relaxed, don’t suck in your stomach.
  • Measure at navel level with a soft tape.
  • Write it down with the date.

Three Metrics to Track Together

  • Scale: Morning weight (judge by weekly average, not day-to-day noise).
  • Waist: Navel measurement once per week.
  • Fit: Belt notch and how shirts/pants fit around your belly.

Even if the scale stalls for a week or two, a shrinking waist means visceral fat is coming down.

3. What Lion + Post-April Could Do for Your Waist

During the 3-Month Lion Phase

  • Start: 210 lb, carrying weight in the midsection (exact waist TBD when you measure).
  • First 4–6 weeks:
    • Big changes in bloat and visceral fat.
    • Possible waist drop: around 2–4 inches, even if the scale is “only” down 10–15 lb.
  • By around April 1:
    • Realistic total loss: 15–25 lb on the scale from 210.
    • Waist likely down about 3–6 inches from wherever you started.

Example: if you began around a 40" waist, being somewhere in the 34–36" range by April is very plausible if Lion adherence is solid.

Post-April Whole Foods + Weekly Fasts

  • Whole Foods only eating pattern.
  • 1× weekly 36–40 hr fast.
  • Slow, sustainable fat loss:
    • Continue trimming midsection steadily.
    • Use the weekly fast as a visceral fat tool and appetite/insulin reset.

Over a few more months, this phase is where you can ride the waist down from the mid-30s into your target 32".

4. How the 170 lb Number Fits In

170 lb is still a useful reference point, but the priority is:

  • Primary target: 32" waist (visceral fat focus).
  • Secondary: body weight number.

You might discover:

  • At 175–178 lb, you’ve already hit a 32" waist and like how you look and feel ⇒ you’re effectively “done.”
  • Or you hit 170 lb but still have a bigger waist than you want ⇒ you keep focusing on waist measurement, not chasing an even lower scale number.
Key idea: Waist wins over weight for your health and how your midsection looks/feels.

5. How to Bias Your Plan Toward Belly Fat Loss

During Lion (Jan → April)

  • 1–2 meals/day, no grazing by default.
  • Walk daily (even 20–30 minutes helps insulin sensitivity and belly fat patterns).
  • Aim for 7–8 hours of sleep when possible (sleep heavily influences midsection fat storage).
  • Don’t use “Lion is clean” as a license to massively overeat:
    • Eat to satisfied, not stuffed.

After April (Whole Foods + Weekly Fast)

  • Keep refined carbs and sugar minimal. Most carbs from:
    • Fruit
    • Vegetables
    • Potatoes / sweet potatoes
    • Occasional whole grains if tolerated
  • Make the weekly 36–40 hr fast standard unless health says otherwise.
  • Use a simple rule:
    • Protein first at every meal to preserve muscle while belly fat drops.

6. Baseline & Milestones: Your “Scoreboard”

Before Starting Lion

Do this once before the Lion phase begins:

  • Morning weigh-in (after bathroom): record as “Lion Start Weight”.
  • Measure waist at navel relaxed: record as “Lion Start Waist”.

Milestone Checkpoints

By April 1 (End of Lion Phase)

  • Target weight zone: likely mid/high 180s.
  • Target waist: around 3–6" smaller than your starting waist.
  • Focus:
    • Looser belt notch.
    • Shirts less snug around the gut.

By June–July (Whole Foods + Weekly Fast)

  • Continue a slow cut (≈ 1 lb/week is fine).
  • Watch waist first, scale second.
  • Goal: glide from wherever you land in April down to a 32" waist.

7. One-Line Summary

The mission is to use Lion (Jan–April) plus a post-April Whole Foods + weekly fast lifestyle to steadily shrink visceral fat until you hit a 32" waist, with the scale as a supporting metric, not the main judge.
